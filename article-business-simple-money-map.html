A Simple Way to Think About Money Every Month

By Daily Writer · 5 min read

Money can feel complicated quickly: budgets, spreadsheets, apps, advice from a hundred places. But most people don’t need a perfect system. They need a simple way to look at their money that makes sense and is easy to repeat every month.

Think in three buckets, not fifty categories

Instead of trying to track every tiny line item, imagine your monthly money in just three buckets:

  1. Must Live – rent, food, basic bills, transport
  2. Must Grow – savings, investments, debt payments
  3. Must Enjoy – fun, treats, little upgrades that make life brighter

Every dollar you earn falls into one of these three. That’s it. Simple enough to remember, clear enough to guide decisions.

1. Must Live: your foundation

This bucket covers the basics that keep your life running: a roof, light, internet, groceries, essential transport, phone. It’s not everything you want, just what you truly need.

A helpful question: Is this something my life actually can’t function without this month? If yes, it goes here. If no, it’s probably in Enjoy.

2. Must Grow: your future

This is the bucket many people skip, not because they don’t care, but because the month runs out before they get to it. Flip that around: try to pay your future self first.

This bucket includes:

  • Savings for emergencies
  • Paying down debt
  • Investing for the long term

Even a small, consistent amount matters. If all you can do this month is a little, do a little. It signals that your future you matters as much as your present you.

3. Must Enjoy: your daily joy

This bucket keeps your life from feeling like a spreadsheet. Coffee with a friend, streaming services, hobbies, small gifts, takeout, a nicer version of something you already use — they all go here.

The trick is to give this bucket a real, honest amount — not zero, not everything. When you give yourself a little room to enjoy your money, it’s easier to stick to your “Live” and “Grow” plans.

One 10-minute check-in per month

Once a month, sit down with your numbers and ask:

  • How much came in?
  • Roughly how much went to each bucket?
  • Do I want to shift anything next month?

You don’t have to be exact down to the penny. The goal is awareness and small adjustments: maybe a little less on Enjoy next month so Grow gets a boost, or maybe you realize Live costs are creeping up and you look for one bill to renegotiate.

Make money a conversation, not a secret

If you share money decisions with a partner or family, try using the three buckets as a simple language: “Can we move a bit from Enjoy to Grow this month?” or “Our Live bucket is getting big — where can we trim?”

Money always feels scarier in the dark. A simple, honest framework turns it into something you can actually talk about.

You don’t have to be a finance expert to take control. You just have to see your money clearly enough to make a few better choices each month. The three buckets are one way to start.
